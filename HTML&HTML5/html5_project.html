My Top 3 Boardgames

Zane Reeder- December 21, 2017
Dominion
This is an amazing card-drafting and deck-building game with a medieval setting. You start with a small hand(kingdom) of money and land, and by using your small resources effectively, your goal is to acquire the most land. This game is great for all 2-4, but 1v1 is definitely its own type game.
Settlers of Catan
An awesome card and board game with resource management as the main mechanic. Everyone starts off small with just two settlements and a few resource tiles to draw from, but by the end cities and roads hundreds of miles long stretch across the newly settled island of Catan. Great for 3 to 4. 1v1 is not recommended.
Dead of Winter
This game starts as cooperative with the colony against the harsh Elements of Winter and Zombies. But there is always the lurking suspicion of a Betrayer among the colony, who secretly seeks its demise. This intense management coop game makes the players feel as though they are in the middle of an apocalypse colony situation. This game is meant for 3-6. But there is a possible 2 player version that is strictly coop without a betrayer. Highly recommend this game for patient gamers that like to play long games. Our average session would last at least two hours.

Texas Tech University Review

Zane Reeder December 21, 2017

Scarlet Red flows through my blood after attending Texas Tech University for four consecutive years. This coming spring semester of 2018 will be last as a Red Raider. I feel I have learned so much about life and my studies in these last four years than I can really comprehend. Texas Tech University is a great school and it's only getting better. Do they spend too much money on athletics? Of course they do, but so does every other college in the south with a decent Division I football team. But, Texas Tech is growing quickly, and the administration is noticing the lack of professors and desire for better learning. The students at Tech are definitely rowdy and love Broadway.
